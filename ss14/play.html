Part 1: So how DO I play SS14, then?

Step 1: Download the game

This should be fairly easy if you know how a computer works.

Head to SS14's website, and click the fairly large Download Now! button.

You've now got 2 choices: (Well, okay, you have 4 choices, but only 2 will work for you.)

    Join the Steam playtest, or

    Download the launcher for your platform.


This might seem like a no-brainer at first, but there's a few things you need to know:

    The launcher doesn't auto update by itself (which is stupid, honestly). The Steam playtest launcher, however, does.

    The non-Steam launcher doesn't have anything to do with Steam, which is nice if you don't like supporting monopolies.

    It takes a LONG time to get your Steam playtest copy.

Also, note the other text on the page. Specifically:

    You need a 64 bit CPU and OS at minimum.

    Windows 7 is NOT supported.


Part 2: I've got the launcher, now what?

You'll now need to make an account. Don't worry, it's not gonna ask for your Date Of Birth, your real name, nor the name of your grandmother.

You'll only need to provide an email, for verification (and to stop spam-bots).

Heck, it'll give you some other cool stuff, like:

    Access to the SS14 forums,

    ...that's about it.


Now, I can't really be bothered to make an account for this guide, but hopefully you'll be able to figure it out from the launcher.

Once you've made an account, you should get a verification email, to activate it.

If you haven't got it, then:

  Make sure you've used the correct email (and made no typos)

  Try resending the email (there's a button, it works)

  Check your spam folder

If all else fails, then it's time to join the discord server and ask for help. There's a link on the website, hopefully you can find it.


Help, the launcher isn't working!

Join the discord and ask for help. Hopefully they'll be able to solve the problem.


Part 3: I'm in! What now?

Click the Servers tab in the launcher. This'll give you a list of every server that's running at the moment.

Then, find one that sounds cool, and click the Connect button.

After a few seconds, you should be in game!

Part 4: Okay, what NOW?

Now, you read the server's rules.

Seriously, read them carefully. Not following them WILL get you banned.


Oh right, I should probably say something about RP types and stuff.

SS14, at its heart, is a roleplay game. You're (probably) expected to roleplay a character, blah blah blah.

There's 4 different RP types you need to know about:

HRP: Hard Roleplay, the strictest of the bunch. Follow the rules to a T, and make sure you read some more stuff, since it'll be important in your journey. Also, be prepared to roleplay. Seriously.

MRP: Medium Roleplay, a step down from HRP. It varies in how strict it is, but know that you're going to roleplay. Also, there's probably some other stuff to read, whatever.

LRP: Low Roleplay, unstrict and chaotic. "Unstrict", however, doesn't mean anarchy. There's still rules to follow, and a little bit of roleplay here and there. Just, don't be expected to have someone follow every important document.

No RP: Simple. You don't roleplay, everyone knows that they're playing a game, and everyone knows what means what. Very chaotic, but remember that it is NOT anarchy. Follow the rules.


If you're playing SS14 for the first time, you're probably either gonna play LRP or No RP.


Uhhh, how do I control myself?

Quick rundown:

  W A S D - Move, like your favourite FPSes (I'm talking about Quake, obviously.)

  E - Interact with the object that's under your mouse cursor

  Q - Drop item in your hand

  Left click - Pick up item, open UIs

  Shift Left click - Examine, you'll be doing this a lot

  Right click - Context menu, a VERY important UI

  I - Inventory (actually, it's what you're wearing at the moment, but)

  F1 - Admin Help


Part 5: Hey, what's Admin Help for?

Glad you convieniently asked that after I mentioned AHelps in the previous paragraph.

Admin Helps (also known as AHelps) are direct messages to the server's admins.

A few things to note:

  Ahelps are used for reporting bad behaviour

  Ahelps can also be used to request stuff, but DON'T expect to be taken seriously, or even listened to.
