how 2: do the whole "survival" thing

(once again: this isn't a definitive guide. you should use a better guide if you want better information.)


So, you want to survive for more than 1 minute.

Uh, are we doing thi-


Step 1: what the FUCK is space?

Space, for lack of a better term, is the area outside our atmosphere that holds nothing. Like, literally nothing.

Because space is, well, space, there's no air. Since we're on a space station (probably), we need to find a way to make an atmosphere: This is atmos's (and thus, the engineer's) job, not yours.


Of course, pobody's nerfect, and atmos (and engineering) can fuck up. When this happens, you'll need 2 (maybe 3 if it's REALLY bad) items:

  • an oxygen tank, to provide the air mixture in a portable format
  • a breath mask (or gas mask), to take the air to your face, and into your lungs
  • an EVA-capable suit, like the EVA suit, Emergency Hardsuit, etc., to allow the lack of pressure to not kill you.

Of course, this is over-simplifying it. Some species don't breath oxygen, and you might not need the suit.

You'll need to activate the internals, too. It's an important distinction between "breathing the air around you" and "breathing the air in this tiny tank"

It should be an action on your hotbar (1-9 buttons on your keyboard). It'll be the one with the gas mask in the corner of the icon.


Step 2: what's this purple stuff that makes it hard to breathe?

If you've been around the station for a fair bit, you might've seen the air look a fair bit purple-ly.

For obvious reasons, this isn't normal.

This gas is known as Plasma (not to be confused with the 4th state of matter, science nerds), and it is VERY dangerous.

If you ever see the air filled with a purple substance, evacuate the area immediately. It's very flammable, very toxic, and is a lawsuit waiting to happen.


Step 3: hey, that green stuff looks cool!

Likewise, if you see a green substance in the air, do not approach it.

That substance, named Tritium, is as dangerous as Plasma, if not more.

Same advice above for this gas.


Step 4: why is someone trying to stab me?

If you ever get into a situation where you're being hurt, remember 3 rules:

Rule 1: if you were the one that hurt someone, you (probably) deserve it.

Rule 2: if the person trying to hurt you is a Security Officer, they're probably a dumbass. Probably.

Rule 3: if you're ever unsure, Ahelp. It won't hurt. Probably.


The only real tips I can give for fighting back are:

  • Run away.

Step 5: The emergency shuttle has docked with the station.

If it's the end of the round, the emergency shuttle will probably be docked to the station.

When that happens, you'll need to board it, less you get left behind when the cleanup operations begin.


Don't worry, all you need to do is to walk through the airlocks, onto the shuttle, and buckle yourself into a chair.

The hard part is surviving the journey home.

Again, don't worry. If you've learnt the above advice about internals and EVA gear, you'll be fine.
